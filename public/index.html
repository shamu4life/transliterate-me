English pronunciations come from the CMU Pronouncing Dictionary (American English); other languages are phonemized by espeak-ng (GPL-3.0), loaded on first use. Transliterations are phonetic approximations — they reproduce how a word sounds, not its meaning or native spelling.

Chinese pinyin uses pinyin-pro, which resolves context-dependent polyphones (长大 → zhǎng dà, 长城 → cháng chéng). Japanese uses kuroshiro + kuromoji for full kanji + kana Hepburn romaji (私は学生です → watashi wa gakusei desu); its ~18 MB dictionary loads on first use. Arabic and Hebrew are abjads — unwritten short vowels can’t be recovered, so those are rough approximations.
